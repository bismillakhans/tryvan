Scope and Outline of the Seminar

There are international principles for governing refugee situations. However, the refugee resettlement records of many countries show that their claim of upholding the principles is hollow when it comes to real refugee situations. The state policies would not reflect on the humanitarian basis of the international principles but will rely on narrow political interests. The injustice behind their forced uprooting from their homelands is not deliberated at the policy level. The refugees share so many similarities with other forced migrants. The present international seminar argues that a convergence of knowledge of the issues faced by the people of both the domains will significantly help in improvising their social, psychological and economic reconstruction in the new region.
